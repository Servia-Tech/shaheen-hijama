How cupping points are actually chosenکپنگ پوائنٹس دراصل کیسے چنے جاتے ہیں

A hijama points chart is a helpful map — but it’s not a recipe to follow mechanically. In practice, an experienced practitioner blends three things: the classical Sunnah points the tradition emphasises, the points associated with your particular concern (say, back pain or headache), and — most importantly — where your own body is tight, sore or tender on the day. Two people with “the same” complaint can need noticeably different placements. That’s why we always examine and listen before we place a single cup.

The classical Sunnah pointsکلاسیکی سنت پوائنٹس

The most emphasised Sunnah location is the upper back between and just below the shoulders — the area around the base of the neck and between the shoulder blades, often described in the tradition as al-kahil. Cupping here is strongly associated with the Sunnah and general wellbeing. Other classically mentioned areas include the two points on either side of the neck/upper back and, in some narrations, points on the head. These are the anchors many practitioners begin from, then adapt. For the tradition behind them, see our article on hijama in Islam.

Points for back & neck painکمر و گردن کے درد کے پوائنٹس

For back and neck pain, cups are typically placed over and around the muscles that are actually tight and sore:

  • Lower-back pain — cups across the muscles either side of the spine in the lower back, around the tender spots.نچلی کمر کا درد — نچلی کمر میں ریڑھ کی دونوں طرف کے پٹھوں پر کپ۔
  • Neck & upper-back pain — around the base of the neck, the tops of the shoulders and between the shoulder blades.گردن و اوپری کمر کا درد — گردن کی جڑ، کندھوں کے اوپر اور شانوں کے بیچ۔
  • Sciatica — following the line of pain from the lower back into the hip, glute and upper leg.عرق النسا — نچلی کمر سے کولہے، سرین اور اوپری ٹانگ تک درد کی لکیر کے ساتھ۔

We go into this in detail in our dedicated guide to hijama for back pain & sciatica.

Points for migraine & headacheمائیگرین و سر درد کے پوائنٹس

For migraines and tension headaches, the commonly used areas are the upper back and shoulders, the base of the neck, and sometimes points on the head or temples — because so many headaches are driven by tightness in the neck and shoulders. Easing that muscular tension is a trusted traditional approach, and many patients tell us their head feels clearer and lighter afterwards. As always, we’ll adjust to where your tension actually sits.

مائیگرین اور تناؤ کے سر درد کے لیے عام طور پر استعمال ہونے والے حصے اوپری کمر و کندھے، گردن کی جڑ، اور کبھی سر یا کنپٹیوں کے پوائنٹس ہوتے ہیں — کیونکہ بہت سے سر درد گردن اور کندھوں کی اکڑاہٹ سے پیدا ہوتے ہیں۔ اس پٹھوں کے تناؤ کو کم کرنا ایک بھروسہ مند روایتی طریقہ ہے، اور بہت سے مریض بتاتے ہیں کہ بعد میں اُن کا سر ہلکا محسوس ہوتا ہے۔

Points for general wellnessعمومی صحت کے پوائنٹس

When people come simply to follow the Sunnah and feel refreshed, the classical upper-back point between the shoulders is the mainstay, sometimes with a couple of supporting points. General-wellness cupping is usually lighter and relaxing rather than intensive, and it’s a popular reason to book on the Sunnah days. If your goal is winding down from stress, our guide on cupping for stress & burnout is worth a read.

Why you shouldn’t rely on a chart to self-treatخود علاج کے لیے چارٹ پر بھروسہ کیوں نہ کریں

Please don’t attempt wet cupping on yourself or a family member from a chart. Wet hijama involves breaking the skin, so it must be done with single-use sterile equipment, correct technique and proper hygiene by someone trained — otherwise there’s a real risk of infection and harm. A chart is for understanding, not for DIY. There are also areas and situations that call for extra care or should be avoided, which an experienced practitioner will know. براہِ کرم چارٹ دیکھ کر خود پر یا کسی گھر والے پر گیلا حجامہ نہ کریں۔ گیلے حجامہ میں جلد کاٹی جاتی ہے، اس لیے یہ سنگل یوز جراثیم سے پاک آلات، درست تکنیک اور مناسب صفائی کے ساتھ کسی تربیت یافتہ شخص سے کروانا ضروری ہے — ورنہ انفیکشن اور نقصان کا حقیقی خطرہ ہے۔ چارٹ سمجھنے کے لیے ہے، خود علاج کے لیے نہیں۔
